Classical Hamburger

Slow-cooked onions add moisture and flavor to these lean beef burgers. A quick blend of mayonnaise, ketchup, relish and vinegar makes a perfect tangy, sweet and creamy “special sauce” for this burger.

Ingredients

1 medium onion, chopped, Yellow Onions 3 Lb
1 pound lean (90% or leaner) ground beef
1 tablespoon canola oil
2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce or steak sauce
2 tablespoons ketchup, divided
½ teaspoon freshly ground pepper
2 tablespoons low-fat mayonnaise
4 sesame-seed or other hamburger buns, toasted
2 teaspoons dill pickle relish
4 slices tomato
1 teaspoon distilled white vinegar
4 leaves green-leaf lettuce

Preparation

1. Preheat grill to medium-high (or see Stovetop Variation).
2. Combine onion, oil and 1 tablespoon ketchup in a medium saucepan.
3. Cover and cook over medium-high heat, stirring often, until the onion is softened, 5 to 7 minutes.
4. Reduce heat to medium-low, uncover and cook, stirring occasionally, until very soft, 5 to 8 minutes more.
5. Transfer to a medium bowl and let cool for a few minutes. Meanwhile, combine the remaining 1 tablespoon ketchup, mayonnaise, relish and vinegar in a small bowl. Set aside.
6. Add beef, Worcestershire (or steak sauce) and pepper to the onion and gently combine without overmixing.
7. Form into 4 patties, about ¾ inch thick. Oil the grill rack (see Tip). Grill the burgers, turning once, until an instant-read thermometer inserted in the center registers 165°F, 4 to 5 minutes per side.
8. Assemble the burgers on toasted buns with the ketchup-mayonnaise sauce, tomato slices and lettuce.
